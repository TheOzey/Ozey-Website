Section II — The name

  • O

    Origin

    Every business starts somewhere — a corner shop, a home kitchen, a WhatsApp group. We build from that starting point, not from an ideal one.

  • z

    Connection

    The informal "z" — the way people actually spell ease in messages. Businesses run on relationships. Our software should feel like one.

  • e

    Structure

    Bringing form to the informal. Not rigid systems, but enough structure to make the work visible — and manageable.

  • y

    Direction

    Every owner is trying to get somewhere. Our job is to clear the path, not add more to carry.

Section III — Why we exist

A small business in India runs across half a dozen surfaces.

A spreadsheet for stock.

A messaging thread for orders.

A printed register for who paid, and who didn't.

A second phone, sometimes, for the bank.

The work isn't missing. The infrastructure is.

Ozey exists in that gap.

Section V — Principles

  1. 01

    Build for the business as it is, not as we wish it were. Most owners don't need a transformation. They need the next hour to be less hard.

  2. 02

    Respect existing habits. WhatsApp, cash, handwritten notes — these aren't failures to overcome. They're signals about what works. Meet people there.

  3. 03

    Make the invisible visible. Subscriptions running in the background. Savings accumulating. Repayments due. Clarity is a feature.

  4. 04

    Earn trust slowly. We're handling money, data, and the business behind someone's livelihood. That demands patience and precision — not growth-hacking.

  5. 05

    Stay focused. Two products, built deeply, are worth more than ten built broadly. We'd rather do fewer things well than many things adequately.
